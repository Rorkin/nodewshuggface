About Ocean Guardian

We are a dedicated alliance of scientists, activists, and ocean lovers committed to protecting marine ecosystems worldwide.

Our Mission

Ocean Guardian is dedicated to protecting and restoring the world's ocean ecosystems. We work tirelessly to combat plastic pollution, protect endangered marine species, and promote sustainable fishing practices.

Founded in 2012, we've grown from a small coastal cleanup group to an international organization with operations in over 80 countries. Our team of marine biologists, environmental scientists, and passionate volunteers work together to create lasting change.

The ocean covers 71% of our planet and produces over 50% of the world's oxygen. Protecting it isn't just about saving marine life—it's about saving ourselves.

Threats to Our Oceans

Understanding the challenges facing our marine ecosystems is the first step to protecting them.

Plastic Pollution

Over 8 million tons of plastic enter our oceans every year, harming marine life and ecosystems. By 2050, there could be more plastic than fish in the sea.

Ocean Warming

Rising ocean temperatures cause coral bleaching, alter migration patterns, and threaten countless species. The ocean has absorbed 90% of global warming's heat.

Overfishing

Over 30% of fish stocks are overfished, disrupting food chains and threatening the livelihoods of billions of people who depend on the ocean for food.

Ocean Acidification

The ocean absorbs about 30% of CO2 emissions, making it more acidic. This threatens shellfish, coral reefs, and the entire marine food web.

Oil Spills

Oil spills devastate marine ecosystems, coating wildlife and contaminating habitats for years. The effects can persist for decades after the initial disaster.

Coastal Development

Destruction of coastal habitats like mangroves and wetlands removes crucial nurseries for fish and natural barriers against storms and erosion.
